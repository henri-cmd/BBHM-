Install & open

It's a Mac app from outside the App Store, so the first open takes one extra step. You only do it once.

MacApple Silicon (M1–M4)
macOS14 Sonoma or later
Size~2 MB download
  1. Download & unzip the file above, then drag BBHM$ into your Applications folder.
  2. Double-click it. macOS shows a warning — click Done.
  3. Open the Apple menu → System Settings → Privacy & Security. Scroll down to “BBHM$ was blocked…” and click Open Anyway, then confirm.
  4. That's it — it opens normally every time after.

If macOS says “BBHM$ is damaged” or there's no “Open Anyway” button: open Terminal (Applications → Utilities), type xattr -cr and a space, drag the BBHM$ app onto the window, press Return, then open the app. This just clears the “downloaded from the internet” flag.

Set up the smart features — optional

BBHM$ works the moment you open it. Add a key to switch on the AI and YNAB features whenever you like.

🤖

Anthropic — read receipts with AI

Without a key, BBHM$ still reads receipts on your Mac. A key adds AI that nails the messy ones — faded thermal paper, odd layouts, handwriting. Pay-as-you-go, a fraction of a cent per receipt.

  1. Go to console.anthropic.com → API Keys → Create Key, and copy it.
  2. In BBHM$, click Settings (the gear) and paste it into the API-key field.
  3. That's it — receipts you add from now on are read automatically.
🔁

YNAB — reconcile against your budget

Connect YNAB to match receipts against the transactions that actually hit your card. It's read-only — it never creates or edits transactions (it can optionally add a colour flag to ones you've reconciled).

  1. In YNAB: Account Settings → Developer Settings → New Token, and copy it.
  2. In BBHM$, click the YNAB button, paste the token, and click Connect.
  3. It's remembered for next time — pick your budget and you're set.

Reconcile against your card

Cross-check your receipts against what was actually charged, so nothing's missing or claimed twice.

🏦

From a bank statement

  1. Export a CSV from your bank, from YNAB, or a Linkt toll history.
  2. In BBHM$, click Reconcile and choose the file.
  3. Review the results (below) and import any that are missing.

Got an old binary .xls? Open it in Numbers or Excel and re-save as CSV first.

📊

Live from YNAB

  1. Connect YNAB (see Setup), then click YNAB → Compare.
  2. Narrow it by budget, category, date range, or flag.
  3. Matched receipts get a green ✓ YNAB tag in your list — and you can flag those transactions back in YNAB in one click.

What you'll see

✅ Matched

Receipt and charge agree — nothing to do.

🚩 Missing receipt

A charge with no receipt. One click adds a placeholder so your claim stays complete.

⚠️ Amount mismatch

Receipt and charge differ — a tip or surcharge, maybe. Flagged for you; the receipt is always kept as the source of truth.

🧾 No bank match

A receipt with no matching charge — perhaps paid another way or on a different card.

💡 BBHM$ learns vendor-name quirks — e.g. a card terminal showing “ZLR*aperitivo” = “Aperitivo Pizza & Pasta”. You can edit these aliases anytime in the reconcile window.

📱 iPhone companion

There's nothing extra to download — it's built into the Mac app.

  1. In BBHM$, click the Phone button in the toolbar. A QR code appears.
  2. Point your iPhone's camera at the QR (phone and Mac on the same Wi-Fi) and tap the link.
  3. Tap Share → Add to Home Screen to keep it as a BBHM$ icon on your phone.
  4. Snap your receipts and tap Send — they appear on the Mac within a second or two, ready to file.

Is it safe?

Short version: yes.

Why the warning?

The app isn't “notarised” by Apple, which needs a paid developer account. The warning is Apple's default for any app from outside the App Store — not a sign anything's wrong.

Where's my data?

Everything stays on your Mac. It only uses the internet for the optional features you set up yourself — Anthropic (to read receipts) and YNAB (to reconcile).

Who's it for?

Anyone who has to turn receipts into an expense claim and is tired of doing it by hand.
